CHAPTER 5
Life Insurance Provisions, Options & Riders
Learn the mandatory and optional provisions built into life insurance policies, including nonforfeiture options, settlement options, dividend options, and common riders.
Payor Benefit Rider
Waives premiums on a juvenile policy if the premium payer (parent/guardian) dies or becomes disabled before the insured child reaches age 18 or 21
Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA) Rider
Automatically increases the policy's face amount each year based on the Consumer Price Index; no new underwriting required
Child Term Rider
Adds term coverage on ALL children of the insured under a single rider; can typically be converted to permanent insurance at specified ages without evidence of insurability
Key Terms
Free Look Period
10 days from policy delivery to review and return the policy for a full premium refund. No questions asked.
Grace Period
30 or 31 days after a missed premium due date. Policy stays in force. If insured dies during grace period, the overdue premium is deducted from the claim.
Incontestability Clause
After 2 years, the insurer cannot contest the policy for misrepresentation or concealment on the application (except fraud).
Reinstatement
Restoring a lapsed policy. Requires evidence of insurability, payment of back premiums with interest, and must be done within 3–5 years of lapse.
Nonforfeiture Options
Options available when a permanent policy lapses. Protects the policyholder's accumulated cash value.
Misstatement of Age
If applicant misstated age, insurer adjusts the death benefit to what the premium would have purchased at the correct age.
Suicide Clause
If insured commits suicide within 2 years of policy issue, insurer returns premiums paid — no death benefit.
Automatic Premium Loan
Optional provision that automatically uses cash value to pay an overdue premium — prevents unintentional lapse.
Core Concepts
Core Concept
Nonforfeiture Options — 3 Choices When a Policy Lapses
When a permanent policy lapses, the policyholder doesn't lose their cash value. They can choose one of three nonforfeiture options:
  • Cash surrender value — take the cash value as a lump sum; policy terminates
  • Reduced paid-up insurance — use cash value to buy a smaller whole life policy; fully paid up, no more premiums, coverage for life
  • Extended term insurance — use cash value to buy term insurance for the original face amount for as long as the cash value will support (DEFAULT option if none selected)
Core Concept
Settlement Options — 5 Ways to Receive the Death Benefit
When an insured dies (or a policy matures), the beneficiary can receive proceeds in several ways:
  • Lump sum (cash) — entire death benefit paid at once (most common)
  • Interest only — insurer holds proceeds and pays interest; principal later
  • Fixed period — proceeds paid over a set number of years
  • Fixed amount — set dollar amount paid each period until proceeds run out
  • Life income — guaranteed income for life; amount based on age and sex of beneficiary
Core Concept
Dividend Options — 5 Ways to Use Policy Dividends
Participating policies (usually mutual company) pay dividends. Policyholder can use them as:
  • Cash — receive dividend as a check
  • Premium reduction — apply to next premium payment
  • Accumulate at interest — leave with insurer to earn interest (interest IS taxable)
  • Paid-up additions — buy additional small whole life coverage (most common/recommended)
  • One-year term — use dividend to buy term insurance equal to cash value
Important: Dividends are a return of overpaid premium — NOT taxable income (until they exceed total premiums paid).
Common Riders
RiderWhat It Does
Waiver of PremiumWaives premiums if insured becomes totally disabled (after 6-month waiting period). Policy stays in force.
Accidental Death Benefit (ADB)Pays double (or triple) the face amount if death is accidental. Called "double indemnity."
Guaranteed Insurability (GIR)Allows insured to buy additional coverage at specified intervals without evidence of insurability.
Accelerated Death BenefitAllows terminally ill insured (life expectancy ≤ 24 months) to receive a portion of death benefit while alive. Tax-free.
Term RiderAdds temporary additional death benefit to a permanent base policy.
Return of PremiumReturns all premiums paid if insured outlives the term. Much higher cost.
Long-Term Care (LTC) RiderPays a monthly benefit if insured needs long-term care; advances the death benefit.

Exam Tip
Default Nonforfeiture Option
If a policyholder does nothing when a policy lapses, the default nonforfeiture option is extended term insurance. This gives the full face amount of coverage for as long as the cash value can buy term insurance. Reduced paid-up gives permanent coverage but for a smaller face amount.
Exam Tip
Dividends Are NOT Guaranteed
Policy dividends are declared annually by the insurer's board of directors and are not guaranteed. They represent a return of excess premium — the insurer charged more than needed and returns the surplus. Because they are a return of premium (not income), they are generally not taxable unless they exceed total premiums paid.
Watch Out
Incontestability Does NOT Cover Fraud
The incontestability clause prevents the insurer from denying a claim after 2 years based on misrepresentation. However, this protection does NOT apply to outright fraud. If an insurer can prove intentional, fraudulent misrepresentation, they can contest the policy at any time — even after 2 years.
Additional Riders — Exam Coverage
Core Concept
Payor Benefit Rider
  • Used on juvenile (child) policies
  • If the premium payer (parent/guardian — NOT the insured child) dies or becomes totally disabled, the insurer waives all premiums
  • Coverage continues until the insured child reaches age 18 or 21 (varies by contract)
  • At that point, the child (now adult) continues the policy and pays premiums
  • Requires evidence of insurability for the payor, not the child
Exam Tip
Payor vs. Waiver of Premium — Know the Difference
Waiver of Premium Rider: waives premiums if the insured becomes disabled (6-month waiting period). Payor Benefit Rider: waives premiums if the premium payer (a DIFFERENT person — typically a parent) dies or becomes disabled. The insured on the policy is the child. The exam will test which rider applies when it's the parent who becomes disabled.
Core Concept
Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA) Rider
  • Increases the face amount annually based on the Consumer Price Index (CPI) or a fixed percentage
  • No new underwriting or evidence of insurability required for automatic increases
  • Protects purchasing power of the death benefit against inflation
  • Premiums increase as the face amount increases
  • If the insured declines an increase for two consecutive years, automatic increases may stop
Core Concept
Child Term Rider
  • A single rider covers ALL children of the insured — does NOT require a separate rider per child
  • Children born or adopted after the policy is issued are automatically covered
  • Term coverage typically lasts until the child reaches age 18 or 25
  • Convertible to permanent insurance at specified ages without evidence of insurability regardless of health
  • Face amounts are typically small (e.g., $5,000–$25,000 per unit)
Watch Out
Child Term Rider Covers ALL Children
One Child Term rider covers ALL children — including future children born or adopted during the policy period. "Does a new baby need a separate rider?" — No. Also: conversion to permanent coverage is allowed WITHOUT underwriting at the specified ages, regardless of the child's current health status.
Quick Recall — Must Know Before Moving On
Free look = 10 days to return policy for full refund after delivery.
Grace period = 30–31 days; policy stays in force; overdue premium deducted from claim if death occurs.
Incontestability = after 2 years, insurer cannot contest — EXCEPT for fraud.
Suicide clause = within 2 years, only premiums returned (no death benefit).
3 nonforfeiture options: cash surrender, reduced paid-up, extended term (DEFAULT).
5 settlement options: lump sum, interest only, fixed period, fixed amount, life income.
5 dividend options: cash, premium reduction, accumulate at interest, paid-up additions, one-year term.
Waiver of premium: 6-month waiting period for disability; policy stays active at no cost.
Dividends = return of excess premium = NOT taxable (until they exceed premiums paid).
Payor Benefit: waives premiums if PREMIUM PAYER (not the insured child) dies or is disabled; continues until insured reaches age 18–21.
COLA Rider: face amount increases with CPI automatically each year; no new underwriting required.
Child Term Rider: one rider covers ALL children including future born/adopted; convertible to permanent without evidence of insurability.
