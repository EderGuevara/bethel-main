CHAPTER 2
Nature of Insurance
Learn the different types of insurers, how they are classified, how insurance is regulated, and the distribution systems agents operate within.
Fraternal Benefit Society
Non-profit organization formed by members sharing a common bond (religious, ethnic, or vocational) that provides insurance to members; operates under a lodge system; some regulatory exemptions
Guaranty Association
State-mandated fund that protects policyholders if an admitted insurer becomes insolvent; funded by assessments on admitted insurers
Managing General Agent (MGA)
Wholesale intermediary with authority to bind coverage, appoint agents, and underwrite on behalf of an insurer; common in specialty and surplus lines markets
Key Terms
Domestic Insurer
An insurance company operating in the state where it was incorporated/chartered.
Foreign Insurer
An insurance company incorporated in one U.S. state but doing business in another state.
Alien Insurer
An insurance company incorporated outside the United States.
Admitted Insurer
An insurer licensed and approved to do business in a particular state. Subject to state regulation.
Non-Admitted Insurer
An insurer NOT licensed in the state but allowed to sell surplus lines coverage for unusual risks.
Fraternal Benefit Society
Non-profit organization that provides insurance to its members based on a common bond (religious, ethnic, etc.).
Reciprocal Insurer
Members insure each other; managed by an attorney-in-fact. Each member is both insured and insurer.
Reinsurance
When an insurer transfers a portion of its risk to another insurer (reinsurer) to limit exposure to large losses.
Guaranty Association
A state fund that pays claims if an admitted insurer becomes insolvent. Does NOT cover non-admitted insurers.
Core Concepts
Core Concept
Types of Insurers
  • Stock company — owned by shareholders, profits go to stockholders
  • Mutual company — owned by policyholders, surplus returned as dividends
  • Fraternal benefit society — insures members sharing a common bond; non-profit; lodge system
  • Reciprocal exchange — members insure each other; managed by attorney-in-fact
  • Lloyd's of London — not an insurer but a marketplace of individual underwriters who each accept a portion of the risk
Core Concept
Insurance Regulation
Insurance is regulated primarily at the state level, not federal. Each state has a Department of Insurance (DOI) headed by an Insurance Commissioner (or Superintendent). The commissioner's role is to:
  • License insurers and agents
  • Approve policy forms and rates
  • Investigate complaints and enforce laws
  • Protect consumers from insolvency
The NAIC (National Association of Insurance Commissioners) coordinates model laws across states but has no enforcement authority.
Core Concept
Distribution Systems (How Insurance Is Sold)
  • Career (captive) agent — represents only one insurer; company's employee or exclusive contractor
  • Independent agent — represents multiple insurers; owns the policy expirations (renewals)
  • Broker — represents the buyer/client, not the insurer; owes fiduciary duty to the client
  • Direct response — sold directly to consumer via mail, internet, or TV without an agent
  • Managing General Agent (MGA) — wholesale agent who distributes for insurers, often for specialty markets

Exam Tip
Domestic vs. Foreign — It Depends on the State
A company that is "domestic" in New York is "foreign" in Massachusetts. The classification is always from the perspective of the state where the company is currently doing business. If the exam asks what type of insurer a NY company is when selling in MA, the answer is: foreign.
Watch Out
Guaranty Associations Only Cover Admitted Insurers
State guaranty associations protect policyholders when an admitted insurer fails. If a client purchases a policy from a non-admitted (surplus lines) insurer and that insurer fails, the guaranty association will NOT pay the claim. Always buy from admitted insurers when possible.
State Guaranty Association — Critical Exam Topic
Core Concept
How the Guaranty Association Works
  • Every admitted insurer must be a member of the state guaranty association
  • If an admitted insurer becomes insolvent, the guaranty association pays claims up to state-set limits
  • Funded by assessments on all other admitted insurers — not by taxes
  • Common limits: $300,000–$500,000 for life insurance death benefits (varies by state)
  • Applies ONLY to admitted insurers — non-admitted/surplus lines are NOT covered
Watch Out
Guaranty Fund Does NOT Cover Surplus Lines
The guaranty association protects policyholders of admitted insolvent insurers only. Surplus lines (non-admitted) carriers are never covered. The fund also has limits — it does not guarantee 100% of every claim in all cases.
Exam Tip
Fraternal Benefit Society
Fraternal societies provide insurance to members sharing a common bond (religious group, ethnic organization, profession). They operate under a lodge system with elected officers. Key distinctions: (1) issue certificates, not policies; (2) NOT subject to all standard insurance regulations — they have some exemptions; (3) non-profit, member-owned structure.
